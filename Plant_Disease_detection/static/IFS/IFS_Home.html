Integrated Farming Guide

Explore sustainable models for Wetland, Gardenland, and Dryland systems.

Wetland-Based Models

Ideal for rice paddies and water-rich areas.

Crop + Fish + Poultry/Duck/Pigeon

Fish control pests in paddies; poultry manure fertilizes water.

Crop + Fish + Mushroom

Mushroom cultivation uses crop residues; fish waste enriches fields.

Rice-Fish Integration

Fish thrive in flooded rice fields, improving soil via excretion.

Gardenland-Based Models

Ideal for horticulture and mixed cropping with livestock.

Crop + Dairy + Biogas

Manure generates biogas; slurry fertilizes crops.

Crop + Dairy + Biogas + Sericulture/Mushroom

Adds silk, fungi, or trees for income diversification.

Horticulture + Duckery + Fishery + Apiary

Ducks control weeds; bees pollinate; compost recycles waste.

Dryland-Based Models

Focus on drought-prone areas with trees and small ruminants.

Crop + Goat + Agroforestry

Goats graze residues; trees prevent erosion.

Agri-Silviculture

Crops under trees for shade and timber.

Silvi-Pastoral/Horti-Pastoral

Trees integrated with grazing livestock or fruit crops.

Broader Integration Types

Livestock-Crop

Animals fed crop residues; manure returns nutrients.

Aquaculture-Agriculture

Fish ponds irrigate and fertilize adjacent crops.

Polyculture / Permaculture

Mimics ecosystems with diverse species for resilience.
